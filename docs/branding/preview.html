Veritra — logo concepts

Ten directions for an open-source, self-hostable, E2EE messenger. Each is shown on light and dark backgrounds. All marks are single SVG files with no font dependencies (except the wordmark text).

01 · Shield V

A shield carrying a negative-space V: privacy first, name second. The safest, most versatile mark.

02 · Bubble + Keyhole

A chat bubble with a keyhole: the most literal “encrypted messaging” read. Tail doubles as the V point.

03 · Verified V

The V’s right arm becomes a bright checkmark — “veritas”, verified identity, delivered-and-authentic.

04 · Sealed Envelope

An envelope whose fold lines draw the V, sealed with a padlock: the server only ever sees sealed envelopes.

05 · App Icon V

A bold geometric V on a dark squircle with an identity-anchor dot — ready to drop in as a mobile app icon.

06 · Chain Link V

Two interlocked links form the V — an end-to-end connection between exactly two parties. Expanded family →

07 · Fingerprint V

Nested chevrons with fingerprint ridge breaks: identity, safety numbers, key verification.

08 · Key V

A key whose bow carries the V — you hold the keys, not the server.

09 · Paper Plane

A folded plane in flat facets: light, fast, self-hosted delivery. The classic messenger metaphor.

10 · Hex Node V

A hexagon badge reading as a self-hosted node on the network, carrying the white V.

Wordmark lockup (concept 01)

Mark + lowercase wordmark. The text uses the system sans as a placeholder; swap in a brand typeface later.
